SeqFX · Stutter Lane · Inspector Redesign

One cut, four ways to edit it.

Static prototypes for the selected-stutter-block inspector. The engine captures one slice and loops that same slice — so the object the user is really tuning is a single per-cut envelope. Each direction visualizes that one cut; the repeat count only shows up as a thin ambient strip. All mockups use the same state: Slices 8, Speed 1.00×, Shape 55 % (bell-leaning), Gate 68 %, Mix 82 %.

What the engine actually does

On block trigger, SeqFx measures the block length in frames, divides by Slices, and captures that many frames of live audio into stutterHistory. It then loops that single captured buffer at Speed for the rest of the block, using a short crossfade (~5 ms or 25 % of the slice) to hide the wrap seam.

Consequence: cuts 2…N are acoustically identical to cut 1. The scarce information isn't where each copy sits — it's what the one cut sounds like. Shape Morph and Gate/Staccato don't exist in the engine yet; they'd promote today's implicit wrap-fade into a tunable per-cut envelope. Slices and Speed already exist and stay.

Direction B

Phase Ring

The captured slice loops — so draw it as a ring. The seam is the top of the circle (where the crossfade already lives). Shape moves the peak around the ring, Gate is an arc-sweep in red, slice count ticks the outer ring.

Tradeoff Honours that the cut is cyclical (loops wrap seamlessly). The gate arc visually "bites" out of the cycle, which reads well for staccato feel. Slice count as ticks scales gracefully from 2 to 32. Cost: radial envelope editing is less familiar than XY, handles are smaller at this diameter, and the gate arc is harder to grab than a linear handle on the edge. Probably a showcase direction rather than the default.

Direction D

Scope + Scrub Bank

Most compact: read-only envelope scope on top, six-icon shape picker, four-cell scrub bank at the foot. All four numbers + a visual of the current cut in under 400 px. No direct manipulation of Shape/Gate on the graph.
