2b · Risograph at SeqFX cell scale

The risograph language compressed into the real grid: 28px cells, 3px gap. Single cells lean entirely on silhouette; 2–3 cell blocks earn a tiny mark; 4–8 cell blocks add readouts. Halftone grain and family colour stay constant across scales.

1 cell · 28×28 · pure silhouette, no labels

Filter — cutoff & mode Crusher — bitrate & hold Tape — direction & curve Stutter — slices & envelope

2–3 cells · silhouette + tiny label

4–8 cells · silhouette + readouts

Filter: each shape is a continuous frequency-response silhouette and the dotted vertical line is cutoff. LP is a filled passband across the left that lifts into a smooth resonant shoulder at cutoff and rolls off down to the right. HP is the mirror — quiet on the left, a smooth resonant rise peaking at cutoff, then a filled passband sweeping out to the right. BP is a smooth bell centred on the cutoff axis; resonance pulls the bell narrower and taller.

Crusher: bitrate sets the number of distinct vertical levels (12-bit = many fine steps; 3-bit = two huge slabs). Resolution / sample-hold sets the width of each step (lower SR = wider, blockier stair-stepping).

Tape: the filled silhouette is the speed envelope over time. FWD reads left→right (mass anchored on the left), REV mirrors. Curve type changes the silhouette shape: linear ramp, convex (EXP), or S-curve.

Stutter: bar count = number of slices (×4 / ×8 / ×16). The envelope of the bar heights reads as the modulation shape (gate / decay / swell). Bar shape (squared, sloped, capped) can encode slice character in larger blocks.
